Facilities

The Château of Sermet has its origins in the XIIIth and XIVth centuries, however the origin of the name Sermet comes from the Sarmatians, a nomadic race originally from Central Asia, 4th Century BC. Several Sarmatian swords have been found in the area and can be seen in archaeological museums.

The Club is set in a building of historic importance, dating back to the 14th Century with 17th century additions, featuring a 34 metre high tower with outstanding views across the French countryside.

Chateau

The Club is surrounded by 140 acres of magnificent rolling parkland stretching all the way down a private valley and a lake with mature woodland and beautiful views.
The Club also offers several km of running trails and hiking paths through fields and wooded areas.

Pool

Adjacent to the Chateau sits a 25 metre long heated salt water pool. We invite all our members to spend time in this area.
Deck chairs and outdoor chaise longues are available upon request, please make sure to ask the Club Manager.

Outdoor dining area

The courtyard seating area is at your disposal for outdoor dining, breakfasts, and even sundowners.

Reception rooms

Several reception rooms are available for meetings, conference calls and general relaxation.

Library

A library with a considerable selection and variety of books is available in the Club, with seating area.

Kitchen

A communal kitchen is available where breakfasts is served every morning. Please make sure to let the Club Manager of any dietary requirements.

Bedrooms & Apartments

Club des Autos offers a substantial choice of ensuite bedrooms in the Chateau and independent self-catered apartments in the Club’s newly restored Grange.

Each room boasts elegant furnishings and all modern comforts you need for a pleasant stay.
